    • Thermochemical cycles

    A thermochemical cycle is a closed cycle that decomposes water into hydrogen and oxygen using heat and a series o chemical reactions. The term cycle is used because aside of water, hydrogen and oxygen, the chemical compounds used in these processes are continuously recycled.

    Drawing a thermochemical cycles

    Example

    Find the enthalpy change for the reation
    NH3(g) + HCl(g) ---> NH4Cl(s)
    The standard molar enthalpy changes of formation of the compound are:
    NH3           ΔfH⦵ = -46 kJmol-1
    HCl           ΔfH⦵ = -92 kJmol-1
    NH4Cl         ΔfH⦵ = -314 kJmol-1

    1. Write an equation for the reaction.
    2. Write down the elements that make up the two compounds with the correct quantities of each.
    3. Put in the ΔfH⦵ values with arrows showing the direction, that is, from elements to compounds
    4. Put in the arrows going from the starting materials to the products via the elements (the red arrows in the diagram).
    5. Reverse the sign of the ΔfH⦵ if the red arrow is in the opposite direction to the black arrows.
    6. Go round the cycle in the direction of the red arrows and add up the values of the enthalpy change as you go.
